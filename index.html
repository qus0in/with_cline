Biography

Søren Aabye Kierkegaard (1813-1855) was a Danish philosopher, theologian, poet, social critic, and religious author. His philosophical work deals with the issues of how one lives as a "single individual," giving priority to concrete human reality over abstract thinking and highlighting the importance of personal choice and commitment.

Key Concepts

  • Subjectivity: Kierkegaard emphasized the importance of subjective truth, arguing that truth is not just something to be known, but something to be lived.
  • Existential Despair: He explored the concept of despair as a fundamental aspect of human existence, arising from the tension between the finite and the infinite.
  • Leap of Faith: Kierkegaard's notion of a "leap of faith" involves a passionate commitment to belief, even in the absence of objective certainty.
